Statistics

ORBITA separates physical transported-volume statistics from provisional path-contact proxies. This prevents the platform from overstating physical moisture contribution before the production moisture-volume engine is complete.

Average transported moisture

Absolute volume will be reported in m³ from transport_volume_m3 or uptake_volume_m3 fields once available.

Relative contribution

Contribution will be reported as %: source/neighbour basin moisture divided by total moisture over retained trajectories and the selected time window.

Agreement and uncertainty

Trajectory agreement is summarized by count and dispersion at each travel time. Thicker mean corridors indicate stronger path agreement.

Methods and Data

Scientific methods behind ORBITA – Orinoquia–Andes Basin Integrated Trajectory Analysis.

a) Basin definition

HydroBASINS Level 6 defines the basin interaction units. The platform displays HYBAS_ID, Pfafstetter code, basin area, and selected-basin attributes.

b) Trajectory computation

The production design uses Lagrangian particle tracking with backward trajectories from basin arrival points or basin grids. The current prototype uses a 2D fixed-pressure RK4 ensemble; the target engine is full 3D RK4 over pressure levels.

c) Atmospheric data

Meteorological forcing is ERA5 reanalysis over South America, including wind components, humidity, pressure-level diagnostics, and surface moisture variables.

d) Moisture transport estimation

Moisture transported along trajectories will be computed from humidity and air-mass/volume diagnostics. Metrics include total volume in m³ and relative contribution in %.

e) Temporal aggregation

Outputs are aggregated over user-defined windows and monthly climatologies. Event-based analysis can be added for specific wet-season or extreme events.

f) Limitations

Uncertainty arises from ERA5 resolution, interpolation, trajectory-model sensitivity, vertical-motion assumptions, and the conversion from path contact to physical moisture contribution.

Eulerian Basin Moisture Budget

Divergence-theorem moisture budget from ERA5 IVT (viwve/viwvn/vimdf), total column water vapor (tcwv), evaporation (e) and precipitation (tp). Sink = P − E = −∇·IVT − d(TCWV)/dt. Positive sink = net precipitation into the basin.
