ORBITA Methodology — Eulerian–Lagrangian Framework with Biotic Pumping Diagnostics

Scientific basis for moisture budget baseline calculation, Net Basin Moisture Injection (NBMI), dry-season green-up paradox detection, and ENSO anomaly attribution.

1. Eulerian Moisture Budget

d(TCWV)/dt = E − P − ∇·(IVT)
NBMI ≡ E − P = −Sink = ∇·(IVT) + d(TCWV)/dt

NBMI = Net Basin Moisture Injection [mm/day]. Positive NBMI = basin exports moisture to the atmosphere (source for flying rivers). Negative NBMI = net precipitation sink. All terms area-averaged over HydroBASINS L6 polygons at 6-hourly ERA5 resolution.

2. Divergence Theorem

∬B ∇·(IVT) dA = ∮∂B (IVT · n̂) ds

Area-integrated moisture flux divergence equals net lateral transport across the basin boundary. Positive convergence (−∇·IVT > 0) = net moisture influx from neighboring regions — the Eulerian signature of flying rivers.

3. Baseline & Multiyear Climatology

NBMIclim(B,m) = (1/N) Σy NBMI(B,m,y)
NBMIanom(B,m,y) = NBMI(B,m,y) − NBMIclim(B,m)

For each basin B and calendar month m, the baseline is the multi-year mean NBMI. The anomaly NBMIanom reveals interannual variability including ENSO. Currently provisional with 8 months of 2023; full 2010–2024 archive enables robust climatology.

4. Dry-Season Green-Up Paradox

NBMIanom > 0 while Panom < 0

Key diagnostic signature. Intact deep-rooted tropical forests exhibit increased transpiration during dry periods and moderate El Niño events — the opposite of what classic hydrology predicts. Documented by Myneni et al. (2007), Bonal et al. (2016), and Maeda et al. (2017). Three mechanisms drive this:

(a) Radiation limitation → water limitation transition. Wet season cloud cover limits photosynthesis. Dry season clears clouds → massive spike in downward shortwave radiation Rg → latent heat flux LE increases → accelerated transpiration (Bonal et al. 2016).

(b) Deep root subsidies and hydraulic redistribution. Primary forest roots reach 10–18 m depth, accessing saturated layers inaccessible to shallow-rooted vegetation. At night, hydraulic redistribution (HR) lifts deep water to dry upper soil horizons via capillary action — boosting dry-season ET by up to 40% (Lee et al. 2005).

(c) Vapor Pressure Deficit (VPD) forcing. El Niño drives up sensible heat → wider VPD → stronger physical "pull" on leaf water. As long as deep roots supply water to prevent xylem embolism, trees transpire heavily, thermoregulating the canopy.

5. Three Pump States

State = f(NBMIanom, forest_cover, drought_duration)

🟢 Active Buffer State: NBMIanom > 0 during low-rainfall/high-heat periods. Intact forest configuration with deep root access. Basin acts as regional climate stabilizer, maintaining moisture supply to downwind Andes even when upstream inflow is dry.

🟡 Critical Water-Stress Threshold: Multiyear mega-drought depletes deep soil water table. Stomatal closure to prevent catastrophic hydraulic failure. NBMIanom abruptly flips from strongly positive → deeply negative (Spanner et al. 2022).

🔴 Degraded/Deforested State: Forest replaced by agriculture or pasture. Shallow roots only — no deep water access. NBMI collapses to zero during drought. Basin loses all capacity to feed flying rivers (da Silva Dias).

6. ENSO Signal Detection

CompositeNiño = mean(NBMIanom | ONI ≥ +0.5)
CompositeNiña = mean(NBMIanom | ONI ≤ −0.5)

Anomalies stratified by Oceanic Niño Index. Intact forest basins are expected to show NBMIanom > 0 (positive injection anomaly) during moderate El Niño — the active buffer response. Degraded basins show NBMIanom ≈ 0 or negative. Basin-level resolution maps which sub-basins are most ENSO-resilient.

7. Lagrangian–Eulerian Integration

The Eulerian budget answers how much moisture enters/exits each basin. Lagrangian trajectories answer where from and how long. Together: (1) Lagrangian source regions cross-validated with Eulerian NBMI sources, (2) time-of-displacement constrains flux attribution window, (3) basin pairs with strong Lagrangian connection AND Eulerian moisture gradient = active flying-river corridors.

8. Sub-Grid Ecohydrological Attribution

Bonus(x,m) = NBMI(B,m) × W(x) / ΣW(x)
W(x) = L(k) × Φ(Z)

Distributed pixel-level weighting using ESA WorldCover LULC classes L(k) and elevation-dependent thermodynamic scaling Φ(Z) = exp(−γZ). Transitions from lumped basin allocation to sub-grid attribution. Captures deforestation impacts: if canopy → pasture, L(k) drops, reducing local moisture injection weight.
